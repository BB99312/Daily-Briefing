Supreme Court Upholds SEC's Authority to Seek Disgorgement in Enforcement Actions

SCOTUSblog

The Supreme Court has validated the Securities and Exchange Commission's use of disgorgement as a remedy in securities enforcement cases, resolving a significant question about the agency's enforcement toolkit. Disgorgement — requiring wrongdoers to forfeit ill-gotten gains — has long been a central mechanism in SEC actions, and the ruling preserves the agency's ability to pursue it in civil proceedings.

Why it matters — The decision has broad implications for securities enforcement and the deterrent power of financial regulators, clarifying the legal basis for a remedy used in hundreds of cases annually.
