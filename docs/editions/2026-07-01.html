Amnesty Documents RSF Crimes Against Humanity in Sudan's El Fasher

World news | The Guardian

Amnesty International has accused Sudan's Rapid Support Forces of committing crimes against humanity and ethnic cleansing during its campaign to seize El Fasher, detailing systematic acts including murder, torture, rape, and sexual slavery against civilians. The report reinforces prior UN assessments that the violence in El Fasher bore the hallmarks of genocide. El Fasher remains the last major city in Darfur not under RSF control.

Why it matters — The Amnesty findings add formal evidentiary weight to genocide determinations and will increase pressure on the international community to respond more forcefully to Sudan's ongoing civil war.

Why it matters — The arrests represent a significant rollback of civil liberties under Niger's junta and highlight the public health risks of criminalizing sexual minorities in countries with fragile healthcare systems.
